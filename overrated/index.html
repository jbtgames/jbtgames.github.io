Overrated

A lovingly sarcastic tribute to the trends that dominate the gaming scene. Tap on a card to discover why these fads deserve a time-out.

Endless Battle Passes

grind fatigue Introduced: 2017 Popularized by: Fortnite & friends

Battle passes promised rewarding progression, but the modern reality is more like a part-time job.

The pressure to log in daily can turn a hobby into a chore. Meanwhile, the rewards often feel like virtual clutter.

  • Artificial fear-of-missing-out tactics.
  • Confusing currencies and tier unlocks.
  • Cosmetics that rarely feel worth the grind.

Ultra-Realistic Mud Physics

tech flex Introduced: 2015 Popularized by: Racing sims

Yes, the mud looks fantastic. No, it does not make the gameplay more exciting.

When studios brag about dirt realism more than track design, maybe priorities need a patch.

  • The novelty wears off after the first splash.
  • Often masks lackluster vehicle handling.
  • Consumes dev time better spent on smarter AI.

Non-Stop Cinematic Cutscenes

passive play Introduced: 2001 Popularized by: Prestige action titles

Games that forget to let you actually play them? A bold creative choice, sure.

Storytelling is great, but skipping 12-minute cutscenes to get back to the action should not feel like a crime.

  • Breaks the pacing and immersion.
  • Disrespects player agency.
  • Unskippable scenes are the real boss fights.
